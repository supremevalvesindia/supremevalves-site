Industrial Valves — Frequently Asked Questions

Valve basics: Gate vs Globe vs Ball vs Butterfly vs Plug vs Diaphragm vs Check vs Control vs Knife Gate

Gate: isolation; Globe: throttling; Ball: quick on/off; Butterfly: compact isolation; Plug: robust for dirty service; Diaphragm: corrosion/slurry-friendly; Check: non-return; Control: modulating; Knife Gate: slurry/solids handling with knife blade.

Material tradeoffs: SS304 vs SS316 vs 316L vs 904L vs Duplex vs WCB vs Brass

Select based on media (chlorides/acid), temperature and weldability. 316L preferred for welding; 904L or Duplex for aggressive chlorides; Brass for potable water; WCB where corrosion is managed via lining/coatings.

ASME B16.5 / ANSI B16.5 flange classes (150/300/600)

ASME B16.5 defines dimensions and pressure-temperature ratings for flanges NPS 1/2–24. Class 150/300/600 refer to pressure ratings dependent on material group and temperature. Facing: RF, FF, RTJ. Gasket selection depends on facing and service.

Flange facing types, bolt patterns, gasket types

Common facings: Raised Face (RF), Flat Face (FF), Ring-Type Joint (RTJ). Bolt patterns follow ASME tables. Gaskets: spiral wound, kammprofile, soft gasket; match to facing and service.

IBR valves: what does IBR certification mean?

IBR applies to steam/boiler service in India. Ordering IBR valves requires approved manufacturers, materials, and inspection documentation. We supply IBR-compliant valves on request with appropriate documentation and TPI as required.

Installation & maintenance

Check flow direction, support heavy valves, align flanges, use appropriate torque, verify actuator ISO 5211 interface, and re-torque after thermal cycles as required.

Testing & inspection

Hydrotest per API/ASME, seat leak tests, PMI, NDT (RT/UT/MT/PT), Third Party Inspection (TPI) coordination with documentation packs.

Export & packing

Export-grade wooden crates, moisture protection, fumigation, and shipping documents (COO, BL, Commercial Invoice, Packing List).
